ISSUE #32 “AROMA 馝”

鸟语花香。。。 Fresh birds, fresh flowers.

Submissions to Sine Theta Magazine's 32nd issue, themed “AROMA 馝” are officially open! Thanks to our Patreon subscribers, Sine Theta Magazine is a paying publication—all contributors will receive a $10 USD honorarium.

After the storm, the morning grass is fresh, green. Mist rises. You spray perfume on the inside of your wrist. Somewhere, a fire is sizzling. The last in our five-senses series comes “AROMA 馝”, an issue unraveling scent in all its pleasant and odious manifestations. Consider the skincare and beauty industry, grass-type Pokémon, baking and brewing, urban and natural landscapes. What captures your nose?

Submissions are due THURSDAY, 18 JULY 2024, at 11:59 PM PST to sinethetamag@gmail.com for full consideration of your work(s). Visit our submissions guidelines for more details. Feel free to DM or email us with any questions you may have; we welcome pitches sent ahead of the deadline!

Those submitting may wish to consider the following prompts:

baking - bread - beer / bong joon-ho’s parasite (2019) / grass-type pokémon (vileplume - roselia - lurantis) / pine - dirt - petrichor - peat bogs / garbage, sewage, and sanitation - urban dwelling / honey lemon tea / farts - shit - ammonia / jasmine - rose - bergamot / tea / top notes - heart notes - base notes - potpourri / 2000s VS perfumes / patrick süskind’s perfume: the story of a murderer / the brooklyn museum mugler retrospective / car leather - cigarettes - weed / gas stoves - cooked rice - roasted sesame / durian / school gymnasiums - athlete’s foot / sweat - musk - pheromones / kink / incense - temples - rituals / bubble baths - skincare / blood - minerality / oil - pollution - energy - climate crisis / must - mold - asbestos / allergies - bees and pollen / phantosmia / hedonic assessment - aromatherapy / the museum of food & drink’s smell synth - the rubin museum mandala lab / alain corbain’s foul and fragrant (1986)

Our contest prompts can also be used for regular submissions! You may submit to both our writing contest and our regular submissions, provided that you submit different work for each. Editors may also decide to consider non-shortlisted contest submissions for our regular issue, and after the shortlist is finalized, we will notify you if this is the case for your submitted work.
