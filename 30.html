ISSUE #30 "COARSE 糙"

皮糙肉厚。。。tough hands, rough skin

Submissions to Sine Theta Magazine's 30th issue, themed “COARSE 糙,” are officially open! Thanks to our Patreon subscribers, Sine Theta Magazine is a paying publication—all contributors will receive $10 USD per piece.

The dregs of ground coffee at the bottom of the mug. The grate of sandpaper against wood. The crunch of gravel beneath bare feet. This issue, we invite you to excavate the sensation of touch with all its pleasures and repulsions, its silk and rubber textures. Explore skin-to-skin intimacy, spin stories of face masks and textiles, or dive into theories of the erotic. We want to grasp what it means to be rough around the edges. Coarse language is, of course, permitted.

Submissions are due SUNDAY, 7 JANUARY 2024, at 11:59 PM PST to sinethetamag@gmail.com for full consideration of your work(s). Visit our submissions guidelines for more details. Feel free to DM or email us with any questions you may have; we welcome pitches sent ahead of the deadline!

Those submitting may wish to consider the following prompts:

the sense of touch / textures - roughness and softness - glossiness and matteness / skin - fur - leather / eczema - dermatitis - allergies / dermatographia ​​- skin writing / tattoos - scarification / wrinkles - scars - scabs - stretch marks - acne - callouses / sandpaper - varnish / nail care - manicures and pedicures - nail salon workers / skin care / sense and sensuality - audre lorde on the erotic - michel foucault on friendship as a way of life / grape stomping / paper-making / natural dyes / dried flowers / braille - tactile exhibits at museums - experiences of blindness and visual impairment / toughness / elasticity / the rubber industry and the history of rubber in colonial contexts / static - electric shocks / textiles - weaving - sewing - knitting - crochet - embroidery / affect and temperament - intimacy - carelessness / pores / sponges - coral - seabeds / crust / bread and breadmaking / stone - asphalt - concrete - brick / reading against the grain / coarse and vulgar language
